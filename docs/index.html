Abstract

We estimate the policy’s Average Treatment Effect on the Treated (ATT) with multiple designs to cross-check conclusions. In the current run, DiD suggests a reduction in incidence (ATT ≈ −245.82, SE ≈ 166.37; not statistically significant at 5%), and PSM suggests a reduction (ATT ≈ −105.20). BSTS was not computed in this environment (R/CausalImpact not available). These preliminary findings point toward a reduction but with uncertainty; we recommend enabling BSTS and/or SDID for triangulation and convergence checks.

Methods

  • Difference-in-Differences (DiD): Two-way fixed effects; parallel-trends assessed with pre-trend diagnostics; region-clustered SEs.
  • PSM: Nearest-neighbor matching on pre-period covariates (see config); balance checked via standardized mean differences.
  • BSTS (optional): Synthetic counterfactual via Bayesian structural time series (requires R & CausalImpact).

Discussion

DiD and PSM both point to a reduction but with different magnitudes and with DiD imprecise. We recommend enabling BSTS (or SDID) to test sensitivity to alternative counterfactual construction, and exploring heterogeneity (e.g., Causal Forests) to identify segments where the effect is strongest or weakest.
