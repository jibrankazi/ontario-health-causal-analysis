Ontario Health Causal Analysis — Policy Evaluation Report

Jibran Kazi · jibrankazi@gmail.com
This report summarizes causal effect estimates using Difference-in-Differences (DiD), Propensity Score Matching (PSM), and (optionally) Bayesian Structural Time Series (BSTS) for a public health intervention.
Contents: Abstract Methods Results Discussion Reproducibility

Abstract

We estimate the policy’s Average Treatment Effect on the Treated (ATT) with multiple designs to cross-check conclusions. In the current run, DiD suggests a reduction in incidence (ATT ≈ −245.82, SE ≈ 166.37; not statistically significant at 5%), and PSM suggests a smaller reduction (ATT ≈ −105.20). BSTS was not computed in this environment. These preliminary findings point toward a reduction but with uncertainty; we recommend enabling BSTS and/or Synthetic Difference-in-Differences (SDID) for triangulation and convergence checks.

Methods

  • Difference-in-Differences (DiD): Two-way fixed effects; parallel-trends assessed with pre-trend diagnostics; region-clustered SEs.
  • Propensity Score Matching (PSM): Nearest-neighbor matching on pre-period covariates (see config); balance checked via standardized mean differences.
  • Bayesian Structural Time Series (BSTS) (optional): Synthetic counterfactual via Bayesian structural time series (requires R & CausalImpact).
